Introduction

ODB+ is a professional-grade automotive diagnostic application for Android. It connects to your vehicle through any ELM327-compatible OBD-II adapter via Bluetooth or WiFi, giving you access to live sensor data, diagnostic trouble codes, and AI-powered repair guidance.

This documentation covers everything from initial setup to advanced terminal commands. Whether you are a weekend DIY mechanic or a professional technician, ODB+ provides the tools you need to understand what your vehicle is telling you.

Installation

ODB+ is available on the Google Play Store. It requires Android 8.0 (API 26) or later and Bluetooth 2.0+ for adapter connectivity.

Requirements

  • Android 8.0 (Oreo) or later
  • Bluetooth 2.0+ or WiFi capability
  • An ELM327-compatible OBD-II adapter
  • A vehicle with an OBD-II diagnostic port (1996+ for US vehicles)

Adapter Setup

ODB+ works with any ELM327-compatible Bluetooth or WiFi OBD-II adapter. For the best experience, we recommend adapters based on genuine ELM327 v2.1+ or STN chipsets.

Bluetooth Adapter Pairing

  1. Plug the OBD-II adapter into your vehicle's diagnostic port (under the dashboard, driver side)
  2. Turn the vehicle's ignition to the ON position (engine running is not required for pairing)
  3. Open your Android Bluetooth settings and scan for new devices
  4. Select your adapter (common names: "OBDII", "Android-Vlink", "ELM327")
  5. Enter the PIN when prompted (usually 1234 or 0000)

WiFi Adapter Connection

  1. Plug in the adapter and turn the ignition ON
  2. Open Android WiFi settings and connect to the adapter's network (usually "WiFi_OBDII" or similar)
  3. Note: WiFi adapters typically use IP 192.168.0.10:35000

First Connection

After pairing your adapter with Android, open ODB+ and navigate to the Connection screen. The app will automatically discover paired Bluetooth adapters or you can enter a manual address. 

Automatic Discovery:
  1. Tap "Connect" on the home screen
  2. Select your adapter from the list
  3. Wait for protocol negotiation (~1-3 seconds)
  4. Dashboard appears with live data

Manual Connection:
  Bluetooth: Enter MAC address (e.g., 13:E0:2F:8D:53:68)
  WiFi/TCP: Enter IP:Port (e.g., 192.168.0.10:35000)

The app automatically detects your vehicle's OBD-II protocol during the first connection. Supported protocols include CAN (ISO 15765), KWP2000 (ISO 14230), ISO 9141-2, and SAE J1850 (PWM and VPW).

Trouble Codes

ODB+ can read and clear Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) stored in your vehicle's ECU. The database includes over 18,000 codes with descriptions, severity levels, and repair procedures.

Code Format

OBD-II codes follow the format XNNNN where:

  • P = Powertrain (engine, transmission)
  • C = Chassis (ABS, steering)
  • B = Body (airbags, A/C)
  • U = Network/Communication
Example: P0171 - System Too Lean (Bank 1)

Category:    Powertrain
Subcategory: Fuel and Air Metering
Severity:    Moderate
Common Causes:
  1. Vacuum leak (intake manifold gasket, hoses)
  2. Faulty MAF sensor
  3. Weak fuel pump or clogged filter
  4. Failing oxygen sensor

AI Assistant

The AI Assistant has access to your vehicle's live sensor data and stored trouble codes. It can answer questions about your vehicle's health, explain diagnostic codes in plain language, and suggest repair procedures.

The assistant references your actual sensor values when providing advice, making its recommendations specific to your vehicle's current state rather than generic.

Terminal Mode

Terminal Mode provides raw access to OBD-II PIDs and ELM327 AT commands. This is an advanced feature intended for users who understand the underlying protocols.

Common Commands

OBD-II Service Commands:
  0100    - Supported PIDs (01-20)
  0120    - Supported PIDs (21-40)
  010C    - Engine RPM
  0105    - Coolant Temperature
  03      - Read Stored DTCs
  04      - Clear DTCs and MIL
  07      - Read Pending DTCs
  09 02   - Vehicle VIN

ELM327 AT Commands:
  ATZ     - Reset adapter
  ATE0    - Echo off
  ATDP    - Display current protocol
  ATRV    - Read battery voltage
  ATI     - Display adapter ID
  ATSP0   - Set protocol to auto

Session Logger

The Session Logger records every command and response during a diagnostic session with precise timestamps. Sessions can be replayed, exported, and shared.

To start recording, tap the record button on any screen. The logger captures all OBD-II communications in the background until you stop it. Session files are stored locally and can be exported as JSON.

Supported PIDs

ODB+ supports all standard OBD-II PIDs defined in SAE J1979 (Services 01-0A). The specific PIDs available depend on your vehicle's ECU. Use the PID discovery feature to see which parameters your vehicle supports.

Compatible Adapters

ODB+ works with any ELM327-compatible adapter. For the best reliability and performance, look for adapters with genuine chipsets rather than clones. Recommended brands include OBDLink, Vgate, and Veepeak.

Troubleshooting

Cannot connect to adapter

  • Verify the adapter is plugged in and the ignition is ON
  • Check that the adapter is paired in Android Bluetooth settings
  • Try resetting the adapter by unplugging it for 10 seconds
  • Ensure no other app is connected to the adapter simultaneously

"UNABLE TO CONNECT" after protocol negotiation

  • The adapter may need a cold reset: unplug for 30 seconds, then reconnect
  • Some vehicles require the engine to be running (not just ignition ON)
  • Try connecting from the Terminal and sending ATZ followed by 0100

Missing or incomplete sensor data

  • Not all vehicles support all PIDs -- use PID discovery to check
  • If you see "NO DATA" for a specific PID, your ECU does not support it
  • Ensure the engine is running for real-time sensor readings
