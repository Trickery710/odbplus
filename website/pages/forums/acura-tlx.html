DCT Troubleshooting with a Scan Tool

The 1st Gen TLX (2015–2020) equipped with the 8-speed dual-clutch transmission (Honda/Acura 8DCT) can exhibit hesitation, shudder, and gear-hunting, particularly at low speeds. Use an OBD-II scan tool to diagnose before authorizing expensive repairs.

Step 1 — Read TCM Fault Codes

  • Connect scan tool and select the AT (Automatic Transmission) or TCM module. Generic OBD-II mode will miss most Honda/Acura-specific transmission codes.
  • P0700 — Transmission Control System malfunction (general flag; look for sub-codes)
  • P0730 — Incorrect gear ratio detected
  • P1738 / P1739 — Shift solenoid valve A/B stuck open or closed
  • P0741 — Torque converter clutch solenoid circuit performance
  • P0868 — Transmission fluid pressure low

Step 2 — Monitor Live Data PIDs

  • Clutch A and Clutch B temperatures — Normal operating range: 70–160 °C. Sustained values above 200 °C suggest slipping clutch packs.
  • Shift solenoid duty cycles (%) — Each solenoid should show smooth, consistent duty cycle ramps during gear changes; erratic readings indicate a worn or stuck solenoid.
  • Input vs. output shaft speed — Compare these during engagement for slip. Any RPM delta during steady-state cruising indicates clutch drag or incomplete engagement.
  • TCM learned/adaptation values — If any clutch adaptation parameter is near its maximum limit, the transmission is compensating for wear and needs service.
  • ATF temperature — If the fluid runs significantly hotter than coolant temperature during normal driving, this may indicate a clogged transmission cooler or degraded fluid.

Step 3 — Perform Engagement Test

  • With the car in a safe, open area, come to a complete stop and watch live clutch temperatures and slip % as you gently accelerate from 0–20 mph in 1st gear.
  • If shudder occurs during this maneuver and clutch slip % spikes, the clutch plates or the transmission fluid are the primary suspects.
  • Perform the same test with A/C off and then on — A/C load changes can expose weak clutch engagement points.

Step 4 — Evaluate and Act

  • Solenoid codes with no clutch temp codes → Inspect/replace the specific solenoid. Often less expensive than clutch work.
  • High clutch temps + high slip % → ATF change first (use only Honda DW-1 or Acura ATF). If shudder persists, clutch pack replacement is required.
  • No codes but consistent shudder → Perform TCM adaptation reset via scan tool after a fluid change so the module relearns clutch engagement points.
  • P0730 (incorrect gear ratio) → Check all speed sensors and wiring harness before condemning the valve body or TCM.

Scan Tool Tip — ODB+ App

Connect an ELM327 Bluetooth or WiFi OBD-II adapter to your TLX's diagnostic port (under the dash, driver side). In the ODB+ app, select AT/TCM from the module list to access Honda/Acura-specific live data and fault codes beyond generic OBD-II mode.
