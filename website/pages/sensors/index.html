Understanding Automotive Sensors

Modern vehicles contain dozens of sensors that monitor everything from engine temperature to wheel speed. These sensors send electrical signals to the vehicle's computer (ECM/PCM), which uses this data to control engine operation, transmission shifting, emissions systems, and more.

Common Sensor Types:

  • Thermistors: Change resistance with temperature (ECT, IAT)
  • Potentiometers: Variable resistors that measure position (TPS)
  • Hall Effect: Magnetic sensors for speed/position (CKP, CMP, VSS)
  • Piezoelectric: Generate voltage from pressure/vibration (knock sensor)
  • Zirconia: Generate voltage from oxygen difference (O2 sensor)
  • Strain Gauge: Measure pressure through material deformation (MAP, fuel pressure)
