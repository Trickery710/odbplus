P0302

Cylinder 2 Misfire Detected

Powertrain Generic (SAE) Severity: High

System

Ignition System / Fuel System

Subsystem

Misfire Detection — Cylinder 2

Common Fix

Spark Plug / Ignition Coil (Cyl. 2)

Repair Cost

$50 - $500+

What Does P0302 Mean?

P0302 indicates that the powertrain control module (PCM) has detected a misfire specifically in cylinder number 2. The PCM monitors crankshaft rotational speed via the crankshaft position sensor and detects the slight deceleration that occurs when a cylinder fails to produce its power stroke. When the misfire rate in cylinder 2 exceeds a calibrated threshold over a set number of crankshaft revolutions, P0302 is stored.

Having a cylinder-specific code like P0302 is a significant advantage over P0300 — it immediately directs diagnosis to the ignition coil, spark plug, injector, and compression health of cylinder 2 specifically, avoiding the need to test every cylinder.

Important: A flashing check engine light means misfires are severe enough to damage the catalytic converter with unburned fuel. Reduce speed and engine load immediately and arrange repairs promptly.

How Cylinder 2 Misfire Detection Works

The PCM identifies cylinder-specific misfires through crankshaft speed analysis combined with the camshaft position signal:

Components Involved in Cylinder 2 Firing:

  • Ignition Coil (Cylinder 2): On coil-on-plug systems, this coil sits directly on the cylinder 2 spark plug. On distributor systems, the distributor routes the high-voltage pulse to cylinder 2 at the correct time.
  • Spark Plug (Cylinder 2): Ignites the compressed air/fuel mixture in cylinder 2 during the compression stroke.
  • Fuel Injector (Cylinder 2): Delivers the metered fuel charge to cylinder 2 on each intake stroke.
  • Crankshaft Position Sensor: Detects deceleration in the crankshaft corresponding to cylinder 2's power stroke interval.
  • Camshaft Position Sensor: Helps the PCM correlate crankshaft position to individual cylinder timing for accurate misfire attribution.

Common Symptoms

  • Rough or Shaking Idle

    One cylinder offline causes noticeable vibration at idle

  • Check Engine Light (Steady or Flashing)

    Flashing indicates severe misfire rate; do not continue driving

  • Loss of Power

    Engine output is reduced proportionally to the number of cylinders affected

  • Hesitation During Acceleration

    Stumbling when pulling away from a stop or during hard acceleration

  • Poor Fuel Economy

    Increased consumption due to unburned fuel and open-loop compensation

Possible Causes

Focus diagnosis on components exclusive to cylinder 2 before investigating engine-wide issues:

Ignition System — Cylinder 2

  • Worn or fouled spark plug (cylinder 2)
  • Failed ignition coil (cylinder 2)
  • Damaged spark plug wire or boot
  • Carbon tracking on coil tower
  • Cracked coil housing

Fuel System — Cylinder 2

  • Clogged or stuck-closed fuel injector
  • Leaking injector (flooding cylinder)
  • Injector wiring or connector fault
  • Low fuel pressure (all-cylinder issue)

Mechanical — Cylinder 2

  • Low compression (worn rings or valves)
  • Burned exhaust valve
  • Head gasket failure at cylinder 2
  • Valve timing problem
  • Carbon buildup on intake valve

Other Causes

  • Vacuum leak at cylinder 2 intake runner
  • Coolant or oil intrusion into cylinder
  • PCM driver fault for coil or injector
  • Crankshaft position sensor fault

Diagnostic Steps

1

Confirm Code and Check for Companion Codes

Use ODB+ to confirm P0302 and note any additional codes. Codes for other cylinders suggest an engine-wide issue (fuel, vacuum). Codes for only cylinder 2 point to cylinder-specific components.

2

Perform the Coil Swap Test

Move the cylinder 2 coil to cylinder 3 (or another adjacent cylinder). Clear codes and test drive. If the misfire code follows the coil (P0303 appears), replace the coil. If P0302 remains, the coil is not the problem.

3

Inspect the Cylinder 2 Spark Plug

Remove and examine the plug. Heavy carbon or black oily deposits, a cracked insulator, or a worn electrode all warrant replacement. Check the gap against manufacturer specification.

4

Test or Swap the Fuel Injector

Listen with a stethoscope for the injector click on cylinder 2. If available, use your ODB+ scanner's injector cut-out test — disabling cylinder 2's injector on a running engine should change idle quality if that cylinder was contributing. Swap the injector to rule it in or out.

5

Compression Test — Cylinder 2

Perform a compression test with all plugs removed. Cylinder 2 compression should be within 10% of the highest reading. Below 100 psi or more than a 20% difference from other cylinders indicates a mechanical problem requiring further diagnosis.

6

Leak-Down Test if Compression is Low

A cylinder leak-down test pressurizes the cylinder and measures how much air escapes. Air heard from the oil cap indicates worn rings; air from the intake indicates an intake valve problem; air from the coolant overflow indicates a head gasket leak.

Common Repairs

Replace Spark Plug — Cylinder 2

Most common repair. Use manufacturer-spec plug type and gap. Replacing all plugs at the same time is recommended if mileage warrants it.

DIY: $5-$25 Shop: $50-$150

Replace Ignition Coil — Cylinder 2

Confirmed by the coil swap test. Replace with an OEM or quality aftermarket coil.

DIY: $20-$80 Shop: $100-$250

Replace Fuel Injector — Cylinder 2

Needed if injector testing confirms a clogged or failed injector on cylinder 2.

DIY: $50-$150 Shop: $150-$400

Engine Mechanical Repairs

Valve job, head gasket replacement, or engine overhaul if low compression is confirmed in cylinder 2.

DIY: $200-$600 Shop: $1,000-$4,000+
